{% if current_category == "Layers"%}

Keeping your own chickens for eggs is both a fun and rewarding hobby. Even children of a young age can be included in this responsibilty and knowing what is in the eggs you eat and where they come from enables you to reclaim some of your food sovereignty

All our laying hen breeds come as point of lay pullets, which is approx. 18 weeks old (breed dependent). At this point, your chickens start laying an egg per day (egg production decreases during winter months. Check out our forum for hacks on keeping up production during the short days).

{% elif current_category == "Broilers" %}

Raise your own meat birds and know exactly what your family is eating! Chickens raised without the excessive use of pharmaceuticals and in your own backyard won't have you second guessing what's in your meat. And being in charge of the welfare of your own animals ensures that your chickens' short lives will be happy.

All our broiler chickens come as off heat broilers.This means theyb require no external heat source and can be kept free range or in outside runs.

{% elif current_category == "Ducks" %}

Ducks make a wonderful addtion to any small holding or homestead. Unlike chickens, ducks don't scratch and their appetite for slugs makes them a great companion to any vegetable patch. While not necessarily requiring a swimming spot (pond, lake, etc.) to be happy, ducks do need a water source to wash their eyes as they don't have eyelids (for more information on ducks check out our forum).

Duck eggs are particular sought after in baking and duck meat can make for rather novel meal.

All our ducks come at point of lay and are or will be producing eggs shortly after purchase.
